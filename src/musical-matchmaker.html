Musical Matchmaker

Web

Being able to compare musical taste can be incredibly useful in a variety of ways: It can help individuals discover new music and expand their listening horizons, people can connect with others with similar taste in music, relationships can blossom over a shared love for a particular artist or a song.

Nowadays it is common to share Spotify profiles or playlists to initiate a conversation around musical taste, but people often have dozens of playlists with confusing names, or gigantic playlists with thousands of songs. This makes it difficult to get a good understanding of a person's musical taste. Musical Matchmaker aims to solve this problem by going through thousands of songs, artists and genres in seconds and creating a convinient report to see how well musical tastes of two people match.

This project is an app approved by Spotify, allowing users to authenticate with their Spotify account which enables the app to download information from Spotify's servers. For the approval every part of the app had to comply with Spotify's rules.

In order to not make excessive requests to Spotify's servers, writing time efficient algorithms was crucial for this project. The back-end of this project is setup as an Node.js Express server, and the front-end is built by using EJS templating language.
